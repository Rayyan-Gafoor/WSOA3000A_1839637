Toxic Power

"True knowledge exists in knowing that you know nothing."~Socrates

I’ve been reading a lot of articles and short stories that revolve around knowledge. Somehow, I always end up asking myself the same question. Is Knowledge ‘toxic’.
We are familiar with the phrase that knowledge is power. And as humans we’ve stumbled upon the fact that power does indeed corrupt.
But does this mean that knowledge will inevitably lead to corruption.
As humans we have a deep and obsessive nature for curiosity.
But what happens when this curiosity leads to knowledge that could possibly lead to one’s own demise.
As seen in religious text as well, Adam and Eve fell victim to curiosity, leading to them being cast out of the Garden of Eden.

One way of how knowledge can be toxic and result in corruption, is when we tend to keep it all to ourselves.
But why do we do this? Keeping the knowledge gives us a sense of ‘power’ and control over those who are unaware of it.
This comes with the fear of being minuteand replaced by those who can understand it better. Either that of its simply because of ones own selfish desires.

In the article, “ As We May Think”, written by Vannevar Bush. He speaks about the ways in which we can share knowledge, and how important it really is.
He continues by saying that we’ve lost so much knowledge over the years, and all this due to poor methods of communication.
Who knows where we would have stood as a race if we had not lost all that knowledge.
But I guess that’s just the side effect that comes with the corruption of knowledge. It tends to slow us down instead of helping us evolve.

But what also makes knowledge so toxic, is the pursuit for it. We tend to drive ourselves insane, becoming obsessive over knowledge we are yet to obtain.
Not everyone is capable of self control. And we tend to go to extreme measures just to have a glimpse of pandoras box. Take the Library of Babel for instance.
The librarians drove themselves crazy, all for the sake of one book, the ultimate form of knowledge.


"Humans invented the atom bomb but no mouse in the world would construct a mousetrap" ~Albert Einstein

What happens once we have this knowledge? We use to find ways to belittle the next man that comes along.
We use it to create weapons against each other, instead of using it to help our race.

Don’t get me wrong, knowledge does not always lead to corruption. I guess in the end, it all depends on ones self-control and what you do with that knowledge.

Zariyah Date: 20 Feb 2020
