Mediums of storing data

In the past, the techonology necessary for storing data was not affordable. Therefore information was both scarce and geographically static. Only a select few were responsible for preserving information. The most common ways of storing information was writting in books. Great Libraries were formed in order to help share and further preserve this knowlegde. Tools were invented in order to further aid the cause. Slowly allowing taking a step forward towards large-scale data presavation. Althpough both preserving and obtaining information required skill, which many at the lacked. Take Epyptian literature for example: Hieroglyphics and hieratic scripts were used to write. Not everyone had the skill required to carve them.

Places of high Information Geographies

Most methods rendered the information it held immobile and geographically static. The methods made it almost impossible to collect and share data. They required skill which took sometime to master. The question where was all this build up of information taking place. Throughout history these places were seen as learning institutions. When we look at the Anglo-saxon history and culture, this just provides more evidence. Monasteries could be found across the entire country. They were seen as a religious and stable learning environments during this time. Although most of the learning, constantly remained confined within the walls of these places. The only means of knowlegdebeing spread form these places were through pilgrims and other travlers that sort shelter.

The monks of these monasteries had certain roles and obligations:

  • First they had the responsibility of maintaining the monasteries.
  • To preserve books of knowlegde and religion, hand copying and painting in monastic scriptorium.
  • To store and preserve books of knowlegde in the monastic libraries

Rise of the Universisties

Today they are seen as the key to mordern education. But what is the history of this place of concentrated knowlegde. Before univerties there were higher educational institutions that consisted of researchers. While they were nkt conducting research they would teach. Aside form these institutions, there were also libraries, in which pupils would gather and are thought by an individual. Although something that aided to the embedment of information, was the fact that only a select few were granted this privilege of learning. One could find more developing places and scholarship throughout specific regions, inducting more and more students. Evantually the Musaeum was founded, which included the great Library of Alexandria. This institution brought information and knowlegde from all corners of the globe. One could say that it functioned as one large univerty of the world. Once it was established that education was the key to ones future, countries all over the world began establishing learning institutions, each using different methods of teaching and knowlegde.
