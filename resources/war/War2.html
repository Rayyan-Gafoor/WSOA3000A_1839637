A Life Worth Living

After a few days of being confined against our will, days that seemed like ages of agony and suffering, I finally felt like I was ready to give up. To this day I still have no idea why I did not. Could it have been the glory and fame that awaited me back home that motivated me, afterall amoung some journalist it is considered as a better motivator than the story itself. All I knew was that at this point, I was willing to kill, if it meant my survival.

I had learned the guards patrol routes, estimating the times, using only but the fraction of light that I recieved from the hole. I was to strike at the second on of them made there way into my cell. Using a small rock I had fashioned into a dull arrow point, days of continuosly grinding, I was finallly ready. I patiently waited for the right opportunity to arise. Then it happened.

At the peak of midnight, a fight broke out. I immediatly thought that the army we were accompanied by had finally come for us. My heart began to race. But then I heard the voices, It was not the amry, no it was rebel forces. One of them ran pass my cell. I called out for help, he hesitated at first, he had a look on his face, a look that scared the living shit out of me. For a second I thought, "do I really want this guy to open this cell?". After a while he turned towards my cell and opened it, all I could think about was: "I was ready to kill one of them, and I am prepared to kill him given the right reason". But no, in the most broken form of english he told me to run, I immediatly thought about the other journalist.

I walked up to her cell, and what I saw still haunts me to this day. For a moment it was like reality hit me, everything around went blank, the gunshot, the screaming, I heard none of it. Why? was all I could bring myself to say. Its funny how life works, the one who had everything waiting for her back home, gone, while I stand here compelety frozen. The urge for me to kill, just grew stronger, and instead of running, like I was told to, I stayed.


Zariyah
